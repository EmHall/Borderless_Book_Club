Borderless Book Club 2020-2021

Travelling From Home

Thank you for expressing an interest in the Borderless Book Club. I have not advertised further, but you are welcome to extend the invitation to colleagues that would enjoy what will hopefully be an inspiring and enjoyable endeavour.

The Purpose

There is so much more to literature than what we have come to consider to be classics. In my research for the reading list I have been astonished by how few works that are considered classics overseas are even in print in the English languages.

I would also like to make more of an effort to read books that put me in someone’s else’s shoes (as Atticus from To Kill a Mockingbird would put it). Not only does this engender respect, but it also engenders respect and compassion… if next time you meet a Russian you can tell them you enjoyed reading Pushkin (instead of making a joke about poison) you will make a friend.

Furthermore, the literary history of every country is unique and fascinating, and as we journey from book to book, you will see that some countries have quite short but powerful literary traditions, some are long but rooted in an oral tradition, and others are founded more on poetry than prose. There is much to discover about literary landscapes before you even open a book.

The Reading List

I have designed this 12-month reading list so that this needn’t be a daunting task. Some months you may have only had time to read the recommended poem, and other months you may have read the Royal Flush: Novel, Short Story, Poem and Classic. I have resisted adding films and non-fiction, but you are welcome to foray into other works and let us know what you discover.

It was very difficult to choose which countries to read, and I didn’t want to opt for regional powers only, but at the same time, it is logical to start with influential countries, and of course I have factored in books beyond their borders so that our impression of each country is as representative as it can be within the constraints of time.

The Meetings

The club will meet on the last Wednesday of every month, starting officially on 30 September. However, I will invite you to a quick introductory meeting on 26 August in case you have any questions, further book recommendations, or requests.

The discussions will orient more around what we have learned about each country (politics, culture etc.), as plot, language and style. I will try to invite colleagues from relevant posts to our discussions, in the hope that they might offer some morsels of insight (please do let me know if you already have the contacts).
